About Me

Hi, I'm Gift, a South African Gameplay and Systems programmer, with a passion for building the foundations that brings games to life. I am currently a gameplay and systems programmer at ItThynk Gaming where I'm developing and implementing systems for an upcoming title. I handle the development pipeline and lead the team in creating scalable systems and tools for the game we're building.


I work alongside team members from multiple disciplines and collaborate on integrating elements into the game. My experience also extends into technical animation, where I work closely with animators to integrate animations cleanly and efficiently. This is an area I enjoy and would like to continue improving as I take on more complex work later in my career.


I enjoy creating the underlying systems that everything else depends on-whether that's player controllers, AI behaviours, event-driven architectures, or tools that empower other developers. For me, the most rewarding part of development is seeing these building blocks come together into a cohesive experience.


Technically, I specialize in designing and implementing state machines, responsive player systems, and flexible event systems that allow different parts of a game to communicate cleanly. I'm particularly drawn to solving around how systems connect, scale, and evolve over time.


My approach to gameplay is rooted in both structure and feel. From a development perspective, I focus on building systems that are maintainable and easy to expand on. From a player prespective, I care deeply about responsiveness , clarity, and depth-creating mechanics that feel good to use while offering room for mastery.


To me, a great mechanic is simple at its core, but versatile enough to create a wide range of outcomes. That balance between simplicity and depth is something I strive for in every system I build.


I'm always looking for opportunities to work on meaningful, engaging gameplay experiences and collaborate with teams that value strong systems design and technical creativity.
